About

Climate Action Hackathon 2025 - Urban Heat & Flood Resilience

The Climate Action Hackathon 2025 Urban Heat & Flood Resilience is a thematic innovation event focused on addressing the growing climate challenges in urban areas, especially rising temperatures and frequent flash floods. The hackathon brings together students, developers, designers, and community innovators to cocreate digital solutions that support SDG 13: Climate Action. Participants will work in teams to build prototypes, apps, or visualization tools that help communities:

  • Mitigate urban heat through green infrastructure mapping, smart shading, or cooling strategies
  • Improve flood resilience by creating early-warning systems, community response tools, or interactive dashboards
  • Raise climate risk awareness using data visualization, storytelling, or geospatial tools.

Workshops, mentoring sessions, and data resources will be provided. The goal is to transform climate data and ideas into practical, community-impactful digital products, encouraging innovation and environmental responsibility.

SDG 13 Connections

How Solutions Reduce Climate Change

  • Heatwave Protection Technologies - Reduces heat-related illnesses and deaths by improving thermal comfort and health safety for vulnerable groups.
  • Temperature Regulation Materials - Decreases urban heat island effects and lowers energy use for cooling, helping mitigate emissions.
  • Hygiene & Water Safety During Floods/Droughts - Ensures safe water and sanitation during disasters, preventing disease outbreaks.
  • Forecasting & Early-Warning Systems - Enables early action and preparedness, reducing casualties and improving health responses during disasters.
